Fong Shing Yee
Athletes have a shorter career than most. Fong Shing Yee had a later start than most other athletes, but giving up has never been an option for him. He shows the world that it’s possible for every athlete to reach the top.
Although a basketball veteran of 18 years, few know that Fong Shing Yee was a football player before switching from the goal to the hoop in secondary school. The power forward worked hard for his dreams despite his late start, and have assembled a collection of awards and championships during his prolific career.
For the past 2 years, the pandemic has not only put a pause on our life, but also on Hong Kong’s top basketball division. But nothing can stop Fong Shing Yee’s passion for basketball. During his extended off-season, Fong kept training and kept learning and kept exploring new possibilities in the game. At the age of 38, he has just started the season in a brand new team. His goal? Reach peak form, get another championship and end his career on the perfect note.
Jordan Lam Lok Kan
Athletes are no strangers to injuries and team changes. Jordan Lam shows the world how opportunities can be created even when uncertainty clouds the future.
Sometimes luck can be every bit as important to athletes as ability. After finishing his football trial in England, Jordan Lam returned to Hong Kong to begin his professional career in 2017. Luck has not been on his side, however, as serious injuries put him on the sideline and his team got disbanded. The series of unfortunate events took a toll on his passion for the sport and made him feel lost. But hitting bottom turned out to be the condition he needed to grow and change.
This season, the attacking midfielder has found a new number to wear and a new team to call family. Proudly donning his new Number 7 jersey, Jordan shows us that despite the hardships facing today’s football industry, opportunities always belong to those who are willing to create them. Even if he has to play a new position, he is prepared to give it everything he’s got.
Hanniel Chow
Through his own two feet, Hong Kong runner Hanniel Chow shows the world what he’s made of — one race at a time.
Hanniel Chow wowed the crowd and made a name for himself with an impressive debut marathon back in 2019. The pandemic has kept him from the podiums of local races since then, but runners always find a way to get faster. This time, he is going to a training camp in Kenya.
Injuries and no racing have made the past two years difficult for Chow, but nothing stops him from going forward. Just last month in the USA, he achieved a new half marathon personal best in a race thanks to a rigorous training regime. But like his impressive marathon debut, he saw it as just a milestone in his running journey. The world is his race course, and he’s running towards his goals one race at a time.
Leung Ka Hin
To many, academic success is the ticket to a bright future. To Leung Ka Hin, however, the future looks very different. He shows the world how a young athlete inspires the future generation.
Faced with a choice between studying and an athletic career, Leung Ka Hin chose the path less taken. Having made his debut in Hong Kong’s top basketball division just 2 years ago, Leung decided to drop out of university this year to take on a brand new challenge — professional basketball.
From playing student division to A1 division, the 21-year old student-turned basketball star has already started his second professional season. It may seem an unlikely path to success, but for Leung it’s the only path. When most people his age think studying is the only worthwhile pursuit, he chose to take a chance. To give it all to his basketball dreams. To get ready for when the time comes to break into the next echelon of professional basketball.
Sun Ming Him
The football field is a place where dreams are made. Sun Ming Him shows the world how he plans to turn the field into his own stage and unleash his brilliance.
From student division breakout star to 4-season veteran in the Hong Kong Premier League, Sun Ming Him’s passion for football never stops burning. Since kindergarten, Sun has been learning and growing with a football under his feet. Having left countless footprints on the field, he still constantly pushes himself to become a better player. He understands that to be cream of the crop, he needs to work extra hard in order to reach world-class level in skills and fitness.
To him, getting into his best shape is the top mission. Because when the time comes, that is how he can make the football field his canvas and unleash his true brilliance.
Leanne Szeto
Every runner wants to run fast. Former professional triathlete Leanne Szeto wants to show the world that every one can run far.
Leanne Szeto lets nothing stop her from moving forward in her sport. The pandemic, however, made competing in local long distance triathlon races or training with other athletes impossible. Not one to give up, she adapted to the situation by giving herself a new goal. She put away the bike and chose to keep going on foot. And now, she’s now going after the marathon.
Most triathlete-turned runners opt for shorter distances like 10k or half marathon. Hungry for a challenge, Szeto went all the way and took on the full marathon. She spends all her time off from work training in order to prepare for her upcoming debut. Between a weekly regimen of 30+ hours consisting of 5am, lunch and after-work sessions, she finds her marathon training even tougher than triathlon. She keeps running towards her goal of sub3 or who knows, perhaps even the podium? You won’t find her complaining. She’s too busy showing the world that every one can run far.
